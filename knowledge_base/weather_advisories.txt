WEATHER ADVISORIES AND CLIMATE-SMART AGRICULTURE
=================================================

## INDIAN MONSOON AND AGRICULTURE

SOUTHWEST MONSOON (Kharif Season):
- Onset: Kerala around June 1; covers entire India by mid-July
- Withdrawal: Northwest Rajasthan around September 1; complete by November 1
- Normal rainfall: 890 mm (June–September)
- Distribution: Northeast India > 3000 mm; Rajasthan < 200 mm
- Impact: 60% of Indian agriculture depends on monsoon

NORTHEAST MONSOON (October–December):
- Affects: Tamil Nadu, AP, Karnataka coastal, Pondicherry
- Provides Rabi season moisture for these areas

## AGROMETEOROLOGICAL ADVISORIES BY SEASON

### KHARIF SEASON ADVISORIES

PRE-MONSOON (April–May):
- Complete land preparation, ploughing, and field leveling before monsoon
- Repair field bunds, drainage channels, irrigation structures
- Arrange seeds, fertilizers, pesticides for timely availability
- Deep summer ploughing (destroys overwintering pests/diseases)
- Sow seeds immediately after onset of monsoon (1st week of June in South; 2nd week of July in North)

ONSET OF MONSOON (June):
- Sow kharif crops within 2 weeks of reliable monsoon onset
- Use moisture conservation techniques (ridges, furrows for dryland crops)
- Apply basal dose of fertilizers at sowing
- Watch for outbreaks of cut worms, cutworms, termites (soil pests active)

ACTIVE MONSOON (July–August):
- Monitor crop growth stages; watch for pest-disease outbreaks
- Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging
- Avoid spraying during rain; spray in dry windows
- Interculture operations during sunny periods
- Top dress nitrogen fertilizer after rain ceases

LATE MONSOON/WITHDRAWAL (September–October):
- Start harvest of early kharif crops (moong, blackgram)
- Prepare for post-monsoon/rabi land preparation
- Harvest crops before unseasonal rain damages mature crops
- Store harvested produce in dry conditions immediately

### RABI SEASON ADVISORIES

PRE-RABI (September–October):
- Test soil moisture before sowing wheat/gram
- Gather seeds, fertilizers, and prepare irrigation schedules
- Prepare seedbeds while residual monsoon moisture is available
- Sow rabi crops in optimal window (see crop-wise schedule)

OPTIMAL SOWING WINDOW (Critical for Yield):
- Wheat (North India): November 1–25 (best); avoid sowing after December 15
- Gram: October 15 – November 15
- Mustard: September 25 – October 15
- Potato: October 10 – November 10

WINTER (November–February):
- Monitor wheat for frost damage at flowering stage
- Cold waves (temperature < 5°C): Cover nurseries with straw/poly cover
- Foggy weather: Reduces evapotranspiration; skip irrigation
- Dew formation: Promotes fungal diseases; watch for rust, powdery mildew

SPRING (February–March):
- Rising temperatures; accelerated crop maturity
- Ensure irrigation at grain filling stage of wheat
- Harvest before hot winds (loo) damage grain quality
- Control terminal heat stress in wheat with light irrigation

## EXTREME WEATHER EVENTS

DROUGHT ADVISORY:
MILD DROUGHT (20–25% rainfall deficit):
- Use moisture conservation: mulching, ridges and furrows
- Apply anti-transpirants (Kaolin 5% spray on leaves)
- Harvest mature crops early; avoid drought-sensitive growth stages
- Switch to drought-tolerant varieties: pearl millet, sorghum, moth bean

SEVERE DROUGHT (> 50% rainfall deficit):
- Claim crop insurance (PMFBY) immediately
- Apply for relief under NDRF (National Disaster Response Fund)
- Plant contingency crops: cluster bean, cowpea (short duration)
- Pray for government SDRF assistance

FLOOD ADVISORY:
BEFORE FLOOD:
- Harvest mature or near-mature crops immediately
- Move harvested produce to higher ground
- Secure farm implements and livestock

DURING FLOOD:
- Do NOT enter flooded fields; electrical risk
- Document crop damage for insurance claim

AFTER FLOOD:
- Report crop damage to district/taluka agricultural office within 7 days
- Drain fields as soon as possible
- Apply potassium nitrate (0.5% KNO3) foliar spray to revive stress
- Replant gaps in paddy/vegetable fields
- Apply additional N top dressing after water recedes
- Apply Trichoderma to prevent fungal diseases in moist conditions

CYCLONE ADVISORY:
- Harvest mature crops before approaching cyclone
- Provide support/staking to tall crops (banana, papaya)
- After cyclone: drainage, plant support, foliar spray of micronutrients

HAILSTORM ADVISORY:
- Report damage to insurance company within 48 hours (for PMFBY localized calamity claim)
- Apply 0.1% Boron spray to promote new growth in damaged crops
- Prune damaged branches in orchards; apply Bordeaux paste on wounds

HEAT WAVE ADVISORY (Temperature > 45°C):
- Increase irrigation frequency; mulch with straw/dry grass
- Harvest night temperatures for transplanting operations
- Protect livestock: provide shade, excess water
- Spray kaolin 5% to reduce leaf temperature in vegetables and fruit trees

## CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION FOR FARMERS

TEMPERATURE RISE IMPACTS:
- Wheat yield: -6% for each 1°C rise above 33°C
- Rice: increased pest pressure; spikelet sterility at > 35°C
- Increase in drought and flood frequency

ADAPTATION STRATEGIES:
1. CROP DIVERSIFICATION:
   - Include heat-tolerant varieties: wheat (K-0307, DBW-187, HD-3385)
   - Rice: Dhan 116, Sahbhagi Dhan (drought-tolerant)
   - Shift to heat-tolerant crops: sorghum, pearl millet, cowpea

2. CHANGE SOWING DATE:
   - Advance wheat sowing 1–2 weeks earlier to avoid terminal heat stress
   - Delay kharif sowing to coincide with better rainfall distribution

3. CONSERVATION AGRICULTURE:
   - Zero tillage or minimum tillage (reduced fuel, moisture conservation)
   - Residue management: mulching instead of burning
   - Cover crops to protect soil from erosion and temperature extremes

4. AGROFORESTRY:
   - Integrate trees with crops (Mango, Neem, Teak)
   - Windbreaks reduce wind erosion
   - Trees moderate field microclimate (2–3°C cooler)
   - Additional income from timber, fruits, fuelwood

5. WATER SMART PRACTICES:
   - Shift from flood to drip/sprinkler irrigation
   - AWD (Alternate Wetting and Drying) in rice
   - Farm ponds for rainwater harvesting
   - Groundwater recharge structures

## CROP WEATHER CALENDARS (NORTH INDIA)

JANUARY:
- Rabi crops: Wheat, mustard in vegetative/flowering stage
- Irrigation: Wheat at jointing; avoid irrigation before cold wave
- Pest watch: Aphids on mustard; powdery mildew on peas
- Advisory: Weed control (hand weeding or herbicide); top dress N on wheat

FEBRUARY:
- Wheat at flowering/heading; critical for yield
- Irrigation: Wheat at milky/dough stage
- Advisory: Foliar spray of Zn and boron on wheat; watch for yellow rust
- Vegetable: Spring maize sowing begins

MARCH:
- Wheat ripening; harvest in last week of March to April
- Mustard harvest; field preparation for summer crops
- Temperature rising; summer vegetable (cucumber, okra) nursery

APRIL:
- Wheat harvest complete by mid-April
- Summer crops (maize, sunflower) sowing
- Land preparation for kharif; deep ploughing
- Advisory: Apply gypsum on sodic soils; lime on acidic soils

MAY:
- Summer crops in growth; hot conditions
- Manage irrigation carefully; irrigate in evening
- Vegetable growing: Apply mulch; increase irrigation frequency
- Advisory: Pre-monsoon land preparation; repair bunds

JUNE:
- Monsoon onset; kharif sowing begins (groundnut, soybean, maize)
- Rice nursery raising; transplanting in June–July
- Advisory: Apply basal dose at sowing; first herbicide application

JULY:
- Rice transplanting; kharif crops in active growth
- High humidity: pest and disease alert (blast, BPH in rice; downy mildew in maize)
- Advisory: Top dress N; scout regularly for pests

AUGUST:
- Kharif crops in peak vegetative/reproductive stage
- Most critical irrigation and pest management month
- Advisory: Apply second split N; monitor for BPH, blast, armyworm

SEPTEMBER:
- Early kharif harvest (moong, urad, fodder crops)
- Late kharif (paddy, cotton, soybean) approaching maturity
- Advisory: Prepare for rabi; soil testing now

OCTOBER:
- Main kharif harvest month (paddy, cotton, maize, soybean)
- Rabi sowing begins (mustard, peas, chickpea)
- Advisory: Use combine harvester where available; stubble management

NOVEMBER:
- Wheat sowing peak period (Nov 1–25: OPTIMAL window)
- Mustard at vegetative stage
- Advisory: Seed treatment essential; basal dose application; first irrigation after CRI

DECEMBER:
- Rabi crops in vegetative stage (wheat tillering, mustard flowering)
- Cold weather; frost risk in hilly areas
- Advisory: Protect nurseries from frost; watch for aphids on mustard

## WEATHER-BASED CROP INSURANCE TRIGGERS

INDEX-BASED CROP INSURANCE:
- Triggers based on weather station data (no crop cutting required)
- Faster claim settlement; more transparent
- Parameters: rainfall deficit, temperature (heat units), wind speed

RAINFALL INDEX:
- Coverage triggers if cumulative rainfall < defined threshold during crop period
- Useful for drought-prone areas where crops fail due to less rain

TEMPERATURE INDEX:
- Covers yield loss due to excess heat (above threshold) or cold waves
- Applied to: wheat (terminal heat), citrus (frost), potato (frost)
