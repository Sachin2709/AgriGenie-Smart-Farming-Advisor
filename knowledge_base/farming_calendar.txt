FARMING CALENDAR AND SEASONAL AGRICULTURAL OPERATIONS
======================================================

## CROP CALENDARS FOR MAJOR INDIAN SEASONS

INDIA'S THREE MAIN CROP SEASONS:
1. KHARIF (Monsoon): June–November; rice, maize, cotton, soybean, groundnut
2. RABI (Winter): October–April; wheat, mustard, gram, peas, lentil
3. ZAID (Summer): February–May; watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber, summer vegetables

## MONTHLY OPERATIONS CALENDAR

JANUARY (Rabi – Peak Vegetative/Reproductive Stage):
WHEAT:
- Irrigate at jointing (65–70 DAS)
- Top dress with N fertilizer if yellowing
- Scout for yellow stripe rust, aphids
- Hand weed if herbicide not applied in December

MUSTARD:
- Protect from aphid attack (>20–25% infestation: spray dimethoate 1.5 ml/L)
- Irrigate at pod-filling stage
- Watch for Sclerotinia stem rot (white rot) in humid areas

POTATO:
- Earthing up (second hilling)
- Irrigate frequently; avoid water stress
- Scout for late blight; protective spray with mancozeb 2.5 g/L every 7–10 days
- Spray chlorothalonil 2 g/L if late blight symptoms appear

VEGETABLE CROPS:
- Harvest peas, spinach, carrot, cauliflower
- Prepare beds for summer vegetables in South India
- Transplant onion (rabi) in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Gujarat

ORCHARD MANAGEMENT:
- Pruning of deciduous fruits (apple, pear, plum) in January–February
- Apply Bordeaux paste on pruning wounds
- Winter spray of lime-sulfur (3–5%) for scale insects and overwintering pests

---

FEBRUARY (Rabi – Flowering/Grain Filling):
WHEAT:
- Irrigate at milky-dough stage (4th irrigation critical)
- Foliar spray: ZnSO4 0.5% + Urea 2% for late yellowing
- Watch for leaf rust appearance; spray propiconazole 1 ml/L

CHICKPEA/GRAM:
- Pod-filling stage; critical irrigation if soil moisture deficient
- Watch for pod borer (Helicoverpa); spray NPV 250 LE/ha or Bt 1.5 kg/ha

SUGARCANE (Spring Planting):
- Prepare field; apply FYM 10–15 t/ha before planting
- Spring planting period begins in February–March
- Plant 2-budded healthy setts; set treatment with fungicide

VEGETABLE SOWING (SUMMER):
- Sow cucumber, bitter gourd, ridge gourd, pumpkin in South India
- Transplant chili, brinjal, tomato for summer crop

---

MARCH (Rabi Harvest, Zaid Begins):
WHEAT HARVEST:
- Wheat ripening; golden yellow colour of straw = harvest time
- Combine harvesting or sickle cutting
- Harvest at 14–16% grain moisture for safe storage
- Threshing, winnowing, bag and store
- Avoid burning straw: incorporate with rotavator

POST-WHEAT OPERATIONS:
- Deep ploughing to destroy pest pupae
- Green manure sowing (dhaincha, sunhemp) for soil enrichment

SUMMER CROPS:
- Sow summer maize, sunflower, summer moong
- Prepare paddy nursery in South India for summer/Boro rice
- Harvest rabi vegetables: cabbage, cauliflower, tomato (early varieties)

---

APRIL (Pre-Kharif Preparation):
PRE-MONSOON LAND PREPARATION:
- Deep ploughing (22–25 cm) after harvesting rabi crop
- Chisel ploughing in black cotton soils to break hard layer
- Apply gypsum (on alkali soils) or lime (on acidic soils)
- Repair field bunds, drainage channels

SOIL MANAGEMENT:
- Soil testing: Collect samples for timely availability of recommendations
- Apply FYM/compost; incorporate into soil before monsoon
- Summer ploughing: Controls weeds, soil-borne pests (termites, white grubs)

SEED AND INPUT PROCUREMENT:
- Book certified seeds of kharif crops in advance (seeds scarce in June–July)
- Procure fertilizers (basal dose): DAP, MOP, SSP, ZnSO4
- Prepare pesticide inventory for kharif IPM

MANGO AND FRUIT CARE:
- Mango fruiting season (April–May in South India)
- Apply Bordeaux mixture 1% for anthracnose prevention
- Irrigate at fruit sizing stage; stop 4 weeks before harvest
- Protect from mango hopper and stone weevil

---

MAY (Last Pre-Monsoon Preparation):
PRE-KHARIF READINESS:
- Complete all land preparation before June 1
- Verify irrigation infrastructure: pumps, channels, drip/sprinkler systems
- Sow green manure (dhaincha) in April-May to incorporate before kharif sowing
- Store water in farm ponds (pre-monsoon rain can partially fill them)

NURSERY PREPARATION:
- Prepare paddy nursery (first week of May in South India; June in North)
- Tomato, chili, brinjal nurseries for kharif season
- Raised nursery beds 1 m wide, 6 m long; treat with Trichoderma + FYM

WATER AND ENERGY MANAGEMENT:
- Check and service pump sets before kharif
- Repair drip/sprinkler system; replace damaged laterals, emitters
- Solar pump installation: May–June ideal time (PM KUSUM scheme)

---

JUNE (Kharif Sowing Begins):
MONSOON ONSET OPERATIONS:
- Sow groundnut, soybean, maize, bajra (pearl millet) with first reliable rain
- Apply basal dose fertilizer (DAP, potash, zinc) at sowing
- Seed treatment mandatory for all crops:
  * Captan or Thiram 3g/kg seed (fungicide)
  * Rhizobium/PSB culture 250g/kg seed (biofertilizer)
  * Imidacloprid 70 WS @ 3g/kg (insecticide for soil pests)

RICE NURSERY MANAGEMENT:
- Raise paddy nursery (June 1–20 for timely transplanting by July 20)
- 25–30 kg seed/ha; nursery area = 1/10 of main field area
- Apply starter fertilizer (5 kg DAP/100 m²) at nursery

WEED MANAGEMENT:
- Pre-emergence herbicide: Apply within 3 days of sowing (pendimethalin, atrazine)
- Power weeder use in row-sown crops from 15–20 DAS

---

JULY (Main Kharif Growth Month):
PADDY TRANSPLANTING:
- Transplant 25–30 day old seedlings (2–3 seedlings/hill; 20×15 cm)
- Apply basal dose 1 week before transplanting
- Maintain 5 cm standing water after transplanting

KHARIF CROP MANAGEMENT:
- First top-dressing of N in maize at V5–V6 stage (knee-height)
- Soybean: first interculture at 20–25 DAS
- Cotton: earthing-up at 45 DAS; apply N split

PEST DISEASE ALERT:
- Rice blast: humid weather; scout regularly; spray Tricyclazole at boot stage
- Maize FAW: check leaf whorl; 5% damaged plants = apply Spinetoram
- Soybean: Yellow mosaic virus vector (whitefly); use reflective mulch, spray imidacloprid
- Cotton: Jassids, whitefly; spray neem-based products first

---

AUGUST (Kharif – Critical Reproductive Stage):
PADDY MANAGEMENT:
- Panicle initiation stage (60–65 DAS): Most critical for yield
- Irrigate adequately; maintain 3–5 cm water level
- Apply third split N at panicle initiation
- Scout for BPH (Brown Plant Hopper): spray buprofezin if > 10 hoppers/hill

COTTON:
- Boll formation stage; most critical for N and K
- Spray 0.2% boron for boll retention
- Scout for bollworms daily in August-September

VEGETABLE HARVEST:
- Harvest monsoon tomato, brinjal, okra, chili
- Cucurbit crops (bitter gourd, ridge gourd) peak harvest

---

SEPTEMBER (Kharif – Harvest Begins):
EARLY KHARIF HARVEST:
- Moong, urad, cowpea: harvest when 80% pods mature; avoid shattering
- Sorghum, pearl millet: harvest at physiological maturity; sun-dry if raining

PADDY:
- Some early duration varieties mature (90–110 days)
- Plan combine hire in advance; combine when 80% grains golden

RABI PREPARATION:
- Soil testing: collect samples immediately after harvest
- Order wheat seed and fertilizers

---

OCTOBER (Main Kharif Harvest + Rabi Sowing Begins):
KHARIF HARVESTING:
- Soybean, groundnut: harvest when leaves start yellowing; 80% pods mature
- Maize: harvest when husks dry; store at <14% moisture
- Cotton: first picking when bolls open 3/4; second picking 20 days later

RABI SOWING STARTS:
- Mustard, lentil, peas: October 1–31 sowing
- Pre-sowing irrigation (palewa) for wheat if no rain
- Apply gypsum/lime if soil correction needed (results take 6 weeks)

FRUIT HARVEST:
- Kharif onion marketing
- Pomegranate: Bhagwa variety harvest in Maharashtra

---

NOVEMBER (Wheat Sowing – MOST CRITICAL MONTH):
WHEAT SOWING OPERATIONS:
- November 1–25: OPTIMAL WINDOW for wheat in North India
- Seed treatment: Vitavax Power (Carboxin + Thiram) 3g/kg seed
- Apply full P, K, Zn as basal dose + 1/3 of total N
- Sow at 100–125 kg/ha; 20–22 cm row spacing

RABI CROP MANAGEMENT:
- Mustard: watch for aphid build-up (November–January peak)
- Chickpea: weed management at 20–25 DAS
- Lentil: Irrigation at branching stage

HORTICULTURE:
- Rabi onion transplanting: Maharashtra, Gujarat, Karnataka

---

DECEMBER (Rabi Crops in Vegetative Stage):
WHEAT:
- Crown Root Initiation (CRI) irrigation: 20–25 DAS after sowing
- Apply second split N at CRI stage
- Watch for yellow rust in humid areas (Doaba region Punjab/UP)

MUSTARD:
- Second irrigation at siliqua filling
- Harvest when 2/3 of siliquae turn yellow

VEGETABLES:
- Cauliflower, cabbage, peas harvest continues
- Tomato, potato crop in growth stage
