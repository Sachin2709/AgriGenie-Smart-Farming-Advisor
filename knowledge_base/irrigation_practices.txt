IRRIGATION PRACTICES AND WATER MANAGEMENT IN INDIA
===================================================

## WATER SCARCITY IN INDIA
India has 4% of world's freshwater but 18% of world's population. Agriculture uses 70–80% of total freshwater. Increasing water productivity (more crop per drop) is critical for food security.

INDIA'S IRRIGATION STATUS:
- Total irrigated area: ~70 million ha (48% of net sown area)
- Major sources: Canals (38%), groundwater (62%), tanks, ponds
- Water stressed states: Maharashtra, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Karnataka, AP, Telangana
- Per capita water availability declining from 5177 m³ (1951) to ~1544 m³ (2021)

## MAJOR IRRIGATION METHODS

### 1. FLOOD/SURFACE IRRIGATION (Traditional)
TYPES:
- Check basin: flat land; tree crops; orchards; efficiency 35–40%
- Border strip: sloping land; field crops (wheat, maize); efficiency 40–50%
- Furrow: row crops (cotton, sugarcane, vegetables); efficiency 50–60%

WHEN TO USE:
- Impervious clay soils with low infiltration rates
- Rice paddy (requires standing water)
- Areas with cheap and abundant water supply
- Traditional farmers with low capital

DISADVANTAGES:
- Very low water use efficiency (35–60%)
- Waterlogging and salinity risks
- Uneven water distribution

### 2. SPRINKLER IRRIGATION
TYPES:
- Portable: farmers move the system; cost ₹40,000–80,000/ha
- Semi-permanent: lateral moves on mainline
- Centre pivot: large scale; grain crops; efficiency 70–80%
- Micro-sprinkler/mini-sprinkler: orchard crops; efficiency 75–85%

SUITABLE CROPS:
- Wheat, maize, groundnut, vegetables, fodder crops, plantation crops
- Sandy soils (cannot hold flood irrigation)
- Undulating topography (cannot channel irrigation)

WATER SAVING: 30–50% compared to flood irrigation

OPERATION:
- Operating pressure: 1.5–3.5 kg/cm²
- Application rate: 5–10 mm/hour
- Irrigate in early morning or evening; avoid windy conditions

GOVERNMENT SUBSIDY: 50–90% subsidy available under PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana)

### 3. DRIP IRRIGATION (MICRO-IRRIGATION)
Most efficient irrigation method; 95% water use efficiency

COMPONENTS:
- Water source → Pump → Filter unit → Fertigation unit → Main line → Sub-main → Lateral lines → Drippers/emitters

DRIPPER TYPES:
- Inline drippers: embedded in lateral; 2–4 LPH discharge
- On-line drippers: plugged into lateral; 2–8 LPH
- Pressure compensating: uniform discharge on slopes

SUITABLE CROPS:
- Vegetables: tomato, pepper, cucumber (60–80% water saving)
- Orchards: mango, citrus, pomegranate, banana, grapes (40–60% saving)
- Sugarcane: 40–50% water saving + 10–15% yield increase
- Cotton: 30–40% water saving
- NOT suitable for: paddy, broadcast crops (wheat, maize in traditional system)

SPACING STANDARDS:
- Tomato/Pepper: 45 cm × 45 cm emitter spacing; 60–100 cm row spacing
- Sugarcane: 1 lateral per 1.5 m row; 50 cm emitter spacing
- Banana: 1 dripper per plant; 2 LPH at 0.7 kg/cm² pressure
- Mango: 4–8 drippers per tree; 8 LPH each

FERTIGATION WITH DRIP:
- Apply 100% P and K as basal
- N in 8–10 splits through drip
- Use water-soluble fertilizers: Urea, MAP, MKP, CAN, SOP
- Venturi injector (no electricity) or fertigation pump
- Flush system after each fertigation

MAINTENANCE:
- Weekly: Check pressure gauges, clogging, leaks
- Monthly: Acid washing (HCl or H2SO4 1%) for mineral deposits
- Seasonal: End-of-season flush; check for rodent damage

COST AND ECONOMICS (TOMATO EXAMPLE):
- System cost: ₹80,000–1,20,000/ha
- Water saving: 50–60% (saves ₹8,000–15,000/ha in electricity)
- Yield increase: 20–30% (₹20,000–40,000 extra income)
- Payback period: 2–3 years
- PMKSY subsidy: 45% for general farmers; 55% for SC/ST/small/marginal farmers

## CROP WATER REQUIREMENTS

CRITICAL IRRIGATION STAGES AND WATER REQUIREMENT:

WHEAT (Total: 400–500 mm; 4–6 irrigations):
1. Crown Root Initiation (CRI): 20–25 DAS – most critical; 50 mm
2. Tillering: 40–45 DAS; 50 mm
3. Jointing: 65–70 DAS; 60 mm
4. Flowering (Heading): 90–95 DAS – very critical; 60 mm
5. Grain filling (Dough stage): 105–110 DAS; 60 mm
6. Milky stage: 120 DAS (light irrigation); 40 mm

RICE (Total: 1200–2000 mm):
- Maintain 5 cm standing water during vegetative stage
- Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD): drain when water level drops 15 cm below surface; refill to 5 cm
- AWD saves 30% water without yield loss
- Drain field 10 days before harvest for mechanical harvesting

SUGARCANE (Total: 1800–2500 mm; 40–60 irrigations):
- Germination phase: light but frequent irrigation (5-7 day interval)
- Grand growth phase (June–September): irrigation at 10–14 day interval
- Maturity phase: stop irrigation 4–6 weeks before harvest

MAIZE (Total: 500–600 mm; 4–6 irrigations):
- Critical stages: germination, 6-leaf stage, tasseling (most critical), grain filling
- Tasseling drought causes 50% yield loss

COTTON (Total: 500–700 mm; 6–8 irrigations):
- First irrigation: pre-sowing (7–10 days before sowing)
- Critical: boll formation and boll filling
- Avoid irrigation during heavy boll opening period

## WATER HARVESTING AND CONSERVATION

FARM POND:
- Size: 30×30×3 m for 10 ha catchment
- Cost: ₹2–5 lakh; government subsidy 50–100% under MGNREGS, RKVY
- Captures 80–100 hours of monsoon runoff
- Provides life-saving irrigation during dry spells
- Can be used for fish culture (additional income)

WATERSHED MANAGEMENT:
- Contour bunding: prevents soil erosion on slopes > 1%
- Check dams: small structures across seasonal streams
- Percolation tanks: recharge groundwater
- Vegetative barriers: grass strips along contours

RAINWATER HARVESTING:
- Rooftop collection: 10 mm rainfall × 100 m² roof = 1000 L water
- Farm pond on 1 ha: captures 400–600 m³ per cm of rainfall

## IRRIGATION SCHEDULING METHODS

SOIL MOISTURE-BASED:
- Tensiometer: measures soil water tension (0–100 centibars)
  * Irrigate when: Sandy soil > 40 cb; Loam > 50 cb; Clay > 60 cb
- Gravimetric method: 100 g soil; weigh before and after oven-drying at 105°C for 24h
- Soil moisture sensor (capacitance type): digital, accurate, expensive

PAN EVAPORATION-BASED:
- Measure daily Class A pan evaporation (Epan)
- Irrigation interval = (Field capacity − current moisture) / (Crop coefficient × Epan)
- Crop coefficient (Kc): wheat = 0.6–1.1; rice = 1.05–1.2; maize = 0.7–1.2

LEAF WATER STATUS:
- Leaf rolling: mild stress indicator in maize and sorghum
- Leaf water potential: precision measurement; < -12 bars indicates stress

## PMKSY (PRADHAN MANTRI KRISHI SINCHAI YOJANA)
OBJECTIVES:
- "Har Khet Ko Pani" (Water to every field)
- "More Crop Per Drop" (micro-irrigation promotion)
- Creating new water sources, distribution network, micro-irrigation

COMPONENTS:
1. Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP): completing incomplete major/medium projects
2. Har Khet Ko Pani: groundwater development, watershed management
3. Per Drop More Crop: micro-irrigation, water use efficiency
4. Watershed Development: conservation and rainwater harvesting

SUBSIDY RATES (Per Drop More Crop):
- General farmers: 45% of project cost (drip/sprinkler)
- SC/ST farmers: 55% of project cost
- Small/Marginal farmers: additional 10% subsidy in many states
- State governments add additional subsidy (total can reach 75–90%)

## GROUNDWATER MANAGEMENT
- Over-exploited blocks in India: 1,186 (16%)
- Critical blocks: 253 (3.5%)
- Semi-critical: 681 (9%)
- Atal Bhujal Yojana: ₹6,000 crore for groundwater conservation in 7 states
- Encourage switch from paddy to less water-intensive crops in Punjab, Haryana
