SOIL HEALTH MANAGEMENT MANUAL FOR INDIAN AGRICULTURE
======================================================

## UNDERSTANDING SOIL HEALTH
Soil health (also called soil quality) is the continued capacity of soil to function as a vital living ecosystem that sustains plants, animals, and humans.

SOIL HEALTH INDICATORS:
- Physical: texture, structure, bulk density, porosity, water holding capacity
- Chemical: pH, organic matter, nutrient levels (N, P, K, micronutrients), EC, CEC
- Biological: microbial biomass, earthworm count, enzyme activity, respiration rate

## SOIL pH AND MANAGEMENT

SOIL pH RANGES:
- Strongly acid: < 5.0 (common in NE India, Assam, West Bengal tea gardens)
- Moderately acid: 5.0–6.0 (suitable for tea, coffee, potato, groundnut)
- Slightly acid: 6.0–6.5 (ideal for most crops)
- Neutral: 6.5–7.0 (optimal for maximum crops)
- Slightly alkaline: 7.0–7.5 (suitable for wheat, cotton, sugarcane)
- Moderately alkaline: 7.5–8.5 (problematic – saline-sodic soils)
- Strongly alkaline: > 8.5 (unsuitable – usar/alkali soils)

ACIDIC SOIL CORRECTION (pH < 6.0):
- Apply agricultural lime (CaCO3): 2–4 tonnes/ha depending on soil texture
- Dolomite lime (CaMg(CO3)2): preferred if Mg also deficient; 2–4 t/ha
- Wood ash: 2–5 t/ha; also provides K and micronutrients
- Apply lime 4–6 weeks before sowing; incorporate into soil
- Recheck pH after 3 months

ALKALINE/SODIC SOIL CORRECTION (pH > 8.0, usar land):
- Apply gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O): 5–10 t/ha for moderately sodic; 10–20 t/ha for highly sodic
- Green manuring with dhaincha (Sesbania bispinosa)
- FYM/compost application: 10–15 t/ha
- Paddy cultivation initially helps reclaim alkali soils
- Pyrite (FeS2): 5 t/ha for calcareous alkali soils

## ORGANIC MATTER AND CARBON MANAGEMENT

TARGET ORGANIC CARBON LEVELS:
- Low: < 0.5% (deficient – most Indian soils)
- Medium: 0.5–0.75% (marginal)
- High: > 0.75% (adequate)
- Ideal: 1.0–1.5%

WAYS TO INCREASE ORGANIC MATTER:
1. FYM (Farm Yard Manure): apply 10–15 t/ha; well-decomposed FYM is best
2. Compost: 5–10 t/ha; NADEP, vermicompost (3–5 t/ha)
3. Green manuring: Dhaincha, Sunhemp, Cowpea – ploughed under at flowering
4. Crop residue incorporation: instead of burning; cover crops in fallow
5. Biofertilizers: Azospirillum, Rhizobium, PSB, VAM (vesicular arbuscular mycorrhiza)
6. Reduced tillage: minimizes oxidation of organic matter

## MACRO NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT

NITROGEN (N) – The yield builder:
- Deficiency symptoms: yellowing from older/lower leaves upwards; stunted growth
- Sources: Urea (46% N), CAN (25% N), AS (20.5% N), DAP (18% N)
- Urea application tips: apply in 3 splits – basal 30%, CRI stage 30%, flag leaf 40% for wheat
- AVOID: broadcasting on flooded fields (volatilization loss), surface application without incorporation
- Recommended rates: wheat 120 kg N/ha; rice 100–120 kg N/ha; maize 150 kg N/ha

PHOSPHORUS (P) – The root developer:
- Deficiency: purpling of leaves; poor root development; delayed maturity
- Sources: DAP (46% P2O5), SSP (16% P2O5), TSP (46% P2O5)
- Apply as basal dose – entire P at sowing/transplanting
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria) can enhance P availability
- Recommended rates: wheat 60 kg P2O5/ha; rice 40–50 kg/ha

POTASSIUM (K) – The quality builder:
- Deficiency: scorching of leaf tips and margins; weak stalks; poor grain quality
- Sources: MOP (muriate of potash – 60% K2O), SOP (sulphate of potash – 50% K2O)
- SOP preferred for chloride-sensitive crops (potato, tobacco)
- Recommended rates: wheat 40 kg K2O/ha; potato 120 kg K2O/ha; banana 300 kg K2O/ha

## SECONDARY AND MICRO NUTRIENT DEFICIENCIES

SULFUR (S):
- Deficiency: yellowing of young leaves (unlike N – starts from top); common in Haryana, Punjab
- Sources: Gypsum (19% S), Elemental S, SSP (11% S), Ammonium Sulfate (24% S)
- Dose: 20–40 kg S/ha depending on crop

ZINC (Zn) – Most widespread micronutrient deficiency in India (nearly 50% soils):
- Deficiency: "Khaira" in rice – brown rusty spots; delayed flowering; small leaves
- Sources: ZnSO4 (33% Zn) – 25 kg/ha as soil application; 0.5% foliar spray
- Seed treatment: 2.5 g ZnSO4/kg seed

IRON (Fe):
- Deficiency: interveinal chlorosis of young leaves; pH > 7.5 soils; waterlogged conditions
- Sources: FeSO4.7H2O – 20 kg/ha soil; 0.5% foliar spray (3–4 times)

BORON (B):
- Deficiency: hollow heart in cauliflower, sunflower; poor fruit set in groundnut, cotton
- Sources: Borax (10.5% B) – 10–15 kg/ha soil; 0.2% foliar spray
- CAUTION: Narrow toxicity-deficiency range; do not over-apply

MANGANESE (Mn):
- Deficiency: interveinal chlorosis; common in light-textured, high pH soils
- Sources: MnSO4 – 20 kg/ha soil; 0.5% foliar spray

## SOIL TESTING INTERPRETATION

SOIL TEST RECOMMENDATION (STR) APPROACH:
1. Collect composite soil sample from 0–15 cm depth (surface) and 15–30 cm (sub-surface)
2. Use clean tools; avoid contamination
3. Mix 20–25 spot samples per hectare
4. Send to nearest Soil Testing Laboratory (under NM-PPHE scheme)

SOIL HEALTH CARD (SHC) SCHEME:
- Government of India issues free Soil Health Cards to farmers
- Tests 12 parameters: NPK, S, Zn, Fe, Cu, Mn, B, pH, EC, OC
- Recommendations specific to crop and soil type
- Renew every 2 years

## SOIL TEXTURE AND CLASSIFICATION

SOIL TYPES IN INDIA:
1. Alluvial Soils (43% area): Indo-Gangetic plains; highly fertile; wheat, rice, sugarcane
2. Black (Regur) Soils (15%): Deccan plateau; cotton belt; high water-holding capacity
3. Red and Yellow Soils (21%): Peninsula; iron-rich; groundnut, millets, tobacco
4. Laterite Soils (8%): High rainfall areas; acidic; cashew, coffee, tea
5. Arid/Desert Soils (4%): Rajasthan; poor organic matter; pearl millet, cluster bean
6. Forest/Mountain Soils (8%): Himalayan region; tea, temperate fruits

SOIL TEXTURE IMPROVEMENT:
- Sandy soils: add clay, FYM, green manure; use water-retaining polymers
- Clay soils: add sand, gypsum; deep ploughing; raised bed cultivation
- Loam soils: maintain with regular organic matter addition

## BIOFERTILIZERS AND SOIL BIOLOGY

TYPES AND USES:
- Rhizobium: legume crops – soybean, groundnut, pulses (seed treatment 250 g/ha)
- Azospirillum: non-legumes – wheat, maize, rice (seed + soil treatment)
- Azotobacter: free-living N-fixer for non-legumes (5 kg/ha soil)
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria): all crops (seed + soil treatment)
- Trichoderma: biocontrol + soil health; 2.5 kg/ha soil application
- VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhiza): phosphorus uptake; drought tolerance

VERMICOMPOST:
- Nutrient content: 2–3% N, 1.5–2% P, 1.5–2% K + micronutrients + humus
- Application rate: 3–5 t/ha; use instead of or alongside FYM
- Benefits: improves aeration, water retention, microbial activity
