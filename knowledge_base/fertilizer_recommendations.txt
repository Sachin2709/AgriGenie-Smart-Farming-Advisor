FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS FOR MAJOR INDIAN CROPS
===================================================

## INTEGRATED NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT (INM)
INM is the judicious use of chemical fertilizers along with organic manures, biofertilizers, and crop residues to maintain soil fertility and maximize yield sustainably.

PRINCIPLES:
1. Soil test-based fertilizer application (STBF)
2. Balanced fertilization – right nutrient, right rate, right time, right place (4R)
3. Combine organic + inorganic sources
4. Use biofertilizers to enhance nutrient use efficiency (NUE)

## FERTILIZER DOSE RECOMMENDATIONS (kg/ha)

WHEAT:
- N:P:K = 120:60:40 (irrigated, timely sown)
- N:P:K = 80:40:30 (rainfed)
- Sulfur: 20 kg/ha as SSP or gypsum
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha if deficient
- Split N: 1/3 basal + 1/3 at CRI (21 DAS) + 1/3 at jointing
- Full P and K at sowing as basal

RICE (PADDY):
- N:P:K = 100-120:40-50:40 (high yielding varieties)
- N:P:K = 60:30:30 (local/medium varieties)
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha as basal if Khaira observed
- Split N: 1/3 basal + 1/3 at tillering + 1/3 at panicle initiation
- Avoid excess N: promotes spindly growth and lodging

MAIZE:
- N:P:K = 150:60:60 (hybrids)
- N:P:K = 80:40:40 (composites)
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha as basal
- Split N: 1/3 basal + 1/3 at knee height (V5-V6) + 1/3 at tasseling (V12)

COTTON:
- N:P:K = 100:50:50 (irrigated Bt cotton)
- N:P:K = 60:30:30 (rainfed)
- Sulfur: 30 kg/ha (critical for quality fiber)
- Boron: 1–1.5 kg/ha foliar (boric acid 0.2%) at bud formation
- Split N: 4 splits – 25% each at sowing, 45 DAS, 70 DAS, 100 DAS

SUGARCANE:
- N:P:K = 250:60:120 (irrigated)
- N:P:K = 150:60:60 (rainfed)
- Sulfur: 30 kg/ha
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha
- Split N: 4–6 splits over the season

SOYBEAN:
- N:P:K = 20:80:40 (starter N only; relies on N-fixation)
- Sulfur: 20 kg/ha as SSP or gypsum
- Rhizobium + PSB seed treatment mandatory
- Foliar urea 2% at pod formation if yellowing observed

GROUNDNUT:
- N:P:K = 20:60:40
- Calcium: 400 kg gypsum/ha applied at pegging stage (critical!)
- Sulfur: 20 kg/ha
- Boron: 1–1.5 kg/ha foliar

POTATO:
- N:P:K = 150:120:120
- Split N: 1/2 at planting + 1/2 at earthing up (30 DAS)
- Calcium: 2–3 kg CaCl2/ha foliar for hollow heart prevention
- Boron: 0.2% borax spray at tuber initiation

TOMATO:
- N:P:K = 100:80:80
- Calcium + Boron: foliar spray to prevent blossom end rot
- Split N: 30% basal + 30% at first flower + 20% at first fruit + 20% at harvest

ONION:
- N:P:K = 100:50:50
- Sulfur: 20 kg/ha (critical for flavor and storage quality)
- Split N: 1/3 at transplanting + 1/3 at 30 DAS + 1/3 at 45 DAS

MUSTARD/RAPESEED:
- N:P:K = 80:40:20
- Sulfur: 30 kg/ha (critical – mustard family requires high S)
- Boron: 1 kg/ha foliar at bud formation

CHICKPEA (Gram):
- N:P:K = 20:40:20 (starter dose)
- Rhizobium seed treatment: 200 g/kg seed
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha if deficient
- Avoid excess N – suppresses nodulation

BANANA:
- N:P:K = 200:60:300 per plant annually
- Split into 8–10 doses (fertigation preferred)
- Micro: Zn (0.5%), Fe (0.5%), B (0.1%) foliar
- Magnesium: 100 g MgSO4/plant if deficiency

## ORGANIC SOURCES OF NUTRIENTS

FYM (Farm Yard Manure):
- Nutrient content: 0.5% N, 0.25% P2O5, 0.5% K2O
- Application: 10–15 t/ha; incorporate 3–4 weeks before sowing
- Enhances soil organic matter, water holding capacity

VERMICOMPOST:
- Nutrient content: 2–3% N, 1.5–2% P2O5, 1.5–2% K2O + micronutrients
- Application: 3–5 t/ha; can partially replace chemical fertilizers
- Safer for soil biology

COMPOST (NADEP method):
- C:N ratio should be 30:1 for good decomposition
- Nutrient content similar to FYM but more stable
- Application: 5–10 t/ha

GREEN MANURE CROPS AND N-CONTRIBUTION:
- Dhaincha (Sesbania bispinosa): 80–100 kg N/ha equivalent
- Sunhemp (Crotalaria juncea): 60–80 kg N/ha
- Cowpea: 40–60 kg N/ha
- Sesbania rostrata: 100–120 kg N/ha

## FERTIGATION RECOMMENDATIONS (DRIP IRRIGATION)

FERTIGATION SCHEDULE FOR TOMATO (per week, per hectare):
- Week 1–2: 5 kg Urea + 3 kg MAP
- Week 3–6: 8 kg Urea + 5 kg MKP + 3 kg SOP
- Week 7–10: 10 kg Urea + 8 kg MKP + 5 kg CAN
- Week 11–14: 5 kg Urea + 10 kg MKP + 8 kg CAN
- pH of fertigation solution: 5.5–6.5
- EC of fertigation solution: 1.5–2.5 dS/m

## FERTILIZER TIMING CALENDAR (NORTH INDIA)

RABI SEASON (Oct–Apr):
- October: Land preparation; apply FYM/compost
- November: Basal dose at sowing (P, K, Zn, 1/3 N)
- December: First split N application at CRI stage
- January: Second split N at jointing; watch for S, Zn deficiency
- February–March: Foliar micronutrient spray if needed
- April: Harvest; stubble management

KHARIF SEASON (Jun–Oct):
- May–June: Land preparation; green manure incorporation
- June–July: Basal dose at sowing/transplanting
- July–August: First split N; pest-disease monitoring
- August–September: Second split N; P-K top dressing if needed
- September–October: Harvest; maintain stubble

## SUBSIDY INFORMATION

GOVERNMENT FERTILIZER SUBSIDY (as per NBS scheme):
- Urea: Fixed MRP ₹242 per 45 kg bag (highly subsidized)
- DAP: MRP ₹1,350 per 50 kg bag
- MOP: MRP ₹1,700 per 50 kg bag
- Complex fertilizers: Variable subsidy under NBS
- Bio-fertilizers: Additional subsidy under Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

SOIL HEALTH CARD (SHC) SCHEME:
- Free soil testing at government laboratories
- Crop-specific fertilizer recommendations on card
- Reduces over-fertilization by 20–30%
- Increases farmer income by ₹5,000–8,000/ha
