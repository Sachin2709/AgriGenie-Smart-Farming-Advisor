PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT IN INDIAN AGRICULTURE
==================================================

## INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM)
IPM is a sustainable approach that combines biological, cultural, physical, and chemical methods to minimize pest damage while reducing risks to people and the environment.

IPM HIERARCHY:
1. Prevention (crop rotation, resistant varieties, sanitation)
2. Monitoring (scouting, pheromone traps, sticky traps)
3. Biological control (natural enemies, biopesticides)
4. Cultural control (field hygiene, timing of operations)
5. Chemical control (last resort; use selective, less toxic pesticides)

ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVEL (ETL):
- The pest density at which control measures must be taken to prevent economic loss
- Apply pesticides only when pest population exceeds ETL

## MAJOR PEST MANAGEMENT

FALL ARMY WORM (FAW) – Spodoptera frugiperda (Maize):
- Identification: Caterpillar with inverted Y on head capsule; 8 spots on abdomen
- Damage: Skeletonizing of leaves; characteristic window pane feeding
- ETL: 5% damaged plants or 1 larva per plant
- IPM Measures:
  * Pheromone traps: 5–10/ha for monitoring; 50/ha for mass trapping
  * Egg mass collection and destruction
  * Release of Trichogramma pretiosum @ 2 lakh/ha
  * Beauveria bassiana 1 × 10⁸ cfu/ml @ 5g/L spray
  * Chemical: Spinetoram 11.7% SC @ 0.5 ml/L; Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC @ 0.4 ml/L
  * Emergency: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4 g/L

BROWN PLANT HOPPER (BPH) – Nilaparvata lugens (Rice):
- Identification: Small brown hoppers; 2–3 mm; both winged and wingless forms
- Damage: "Hopper burn" – circular patches of dry brown plants; honeydew promotes sooty mold
- ETL: 10–15 hoppers/hill or 1000/m²
- IPM Measures:
  * Resistant varieties: Swarna Sub1, IR-64, TDK-1
  * Avoid excess N application
  * Keep drain channels clear
  * Spray buprofezin 25% SC @ 1.25 ml/L; imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/L
  * Biological: Mirid bugs, spiders as natural enemies

PINK BOLLWORM (PBW) – Pectinophora gossypiella (Cotton):
- Identification: Pink colored larva inside bolls; rosette flower
- Damage: Feeding on seeds inside bolls; reduces lint quality
- ETL: 8–10% rosette flowers or 2 moths/trap/day
- IPM:
  * Bt cotton varieties have built-in resistance (Cry1Ac, Cry2Ab)
  * Pheromone traps: 10–15/ha for monitoring
  * Sex pheromone confusion technique in heavy infestations
  * Chlorpyrifos 20% EC @ 2.5 ml/L for severe infestation

WHITEFLY – Bemisia tabaci (Cotton, Vegetables):
- Identification: Small white winged insects on leaf undersides; excretes honeydew
- Damage: Direct sap sucking; indirect – transmits yellow mosaic virus (YMV) in soybean
- ETL: 6–10 adults/leaf
- IPM:
  * Yellow sticky traps: 25/ha for monitoring
  * Reflective mulch deters landing
  * Neem-based pesticides: Azadirachtin 1% @ 3–5 ml/L
  * Chemical: Thiamethoxam 25% WG @ 0.3 g/L; Spiromesifen 22.9% SC @ 0.9 ml/L
  * Avoid: repeated use of neonicotinoids (resistance risk)

APHIDS – Multiple species (Various crops):
- Damage: Sap sucking; honeydew leads to sooty mold; virus transmission
- Natural control: Ladybird beetles (Coccinella septempunctata), lacewings
- Chemical: Dimethoate 30% EC @ 1.5 ml/L; Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/L

STEM BORERS (Rice, Maize):
- Shoot fly (Atherigona soccata – Sorghum): Dead heart in young plants
- Yellow stem borer (Scirpophaga incertulas – Rice): Dead heart + white ear
- IPM: Trichogramma egg parasitoid release; Pheromone traps; Chlorpyrifos granules in leaf whorl

AMERICAN BOLLWORM (ABW) – Helicoverpa armigera (Cotton, Tomato, Chickpea):
- Most polyphagous pest in India; attacks 180+ crops
- Damage: Boring into fruiting bodies; circular feeding holes
- ETL: 5 larvae/10 plants (cotton); 2 larvae/m row (tomato)
- IPM:
  * NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus): 250 LE/ha sprayed at egg hatching
  * Trichogramma: 2 lakh/ha release at egg laying
  * Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) formulations: 1.5 kg/ha
  * Chemical: Indoxacarb 14.5% SC @ 0.8 ml/L; Emamectin benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4 g/L

## MAJOR DISEASE MANAGEMENT

WHEAT RUST (Leaf, Stem, Stripe):
- Causal organism: Puccinia spp. (fungi)
- Symptoms:
  * Leaf rust (P. triticina): Orange-brown uredinia on leaves
  * Stem rust (P. graminis): Large dark brown-black pustules on stem
  * Stripe/Yellow rust (P. striiformis): Yellow-stripe pustules on leaves
- Management:
  * Resistant varieties: HD-2967 (leaf, stem resistant); HD-3086, WH-1105
  * Seed treatment: Carboxin 37.5% + Thiram 37.5% DS @ 3g/kg seed
  * Foliar spray at first sign: Propiconazole 25% EC @ 1 ml/L (2–3 sprays, 15-day interval)
  * Tebuconazole 25.9% EW @ 1 ml/L

RICE BLAST – Magnaporthe oryzae:
- Symptoms: Diamond/spindle-shaped lesions with gray center and brown border on leaves; neck blast (panicle emergence most critical)
- Favored by: 25–30°C; high humidity; excess N; heavy dew
- Management:
  * Resistant varieties: Improved Samba Mahsuri, MTU-7029
  * Balanced N fertilization (avoid excess)
  * Tricyclazole 75% WP @ 0.6 g/L at boot-leaf and neck emergence
  * Isoprothiolane 40% EC @ 1.5 ml/L

LATE BLIGHT (Potato, Tomato) – Phytophthora infestans:
- Most devastating plant disease worldwide; Irish famine (1845)
- Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions rapidly turning brown-black; white mold on leaf undersides in humid conditions
- Favored by: 15–20°C; >90% RH; leaf wetness > 10 hours
- Management:
  * Certified disease-free seed material
  * Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L (protectant; start before disease appearance in monsoon)
  * Metalaxyl + Mancozeb 72% WP @ 2.5 g/L (systemic + contact)
  * Cymoxanil + Mancozeb 72% WP @ 3 g/L
  * Avoid overhead irrigation; maintain air circulation
  * 7–10 day spray interval during favorable weather

BACTERIAL WILT – Ralstonia solanacearum (Tomato, Potato, Brinjal):
- Symptoms: Sudden wilting of plants without yellowing; white-milky bacterial ooze in water from cut stem
- Soil-borne; spreads through water and tools
- Management (NO effective chemical cure):
  * Resistant varieties: Arka Vikas, Arka Rakshak (tomato)
  * Crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops (4–5 years)
  * Soil solarization: 6–8 weeks under plastic mulch in May–June
  * Biofumigation with brassica crops
  * Trichoderma viride @ 2.5 kg/ha soil treatment

POWDERY MILDEW (Various crops):
- Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves, stems, fruits; poor photosynthesis
- Management:
  * Wettable sulfur 80% WP @ 3 g/L (most effective for cucurbits, grapes, rose)
  * Hexaconazole 5% EC @ 2 ml/L
  * Karathane (Dinocap) 48% EC @ 1 ml/L
  * Spray in morning; avoid evening sprays in cold weather

## BIOPESTICIDES AND ORGANIC PEST MANAGEMENT

NEEM-BASED PESTICIDES:
- Azadirachtin 0.15% EC (300 ppm) – 5 ml/L
- Azadirachtin 0.3% EC (1500 ppm) – 3 ml/L
- Neem cake: 250 kg/ha soil incorporation for soil pests and nematodes
- Mode of action: Anti-feedant, growth regulator, ovipositional deterrent
- Best against: Sucking pests, leaf miners, whitefly, thrips, jassids

BACILLUS THURINGIENSIS (Bt):
- Bt var. kurstaki: Caterpillars (lepidopterous pests)
- Bt var. israelensis: Mosquito, fungus gnat larvae
- Formulation: WP, SC, GR; apply at early instar stage
- Dose: 1–2 kg/ha (WP); best in evening or cloudy conditions

BEAUVERIA BASSIANA:
- Entomopathogenic fungus; infects wide range of pests
- Effective against: FAW, thrips, whitefly, aphids, grasshoppers
- Dose: 5 g/L (1 × 10⁸ cfu/g); spray in evening; avoid UV exposure
- Store in cool, dark conditions

TRICHODERMA VIRIDE / T. HARZIANUM:
- Biocontrol agent for soil-borne diseases
- Effective against: Fusarium, Pythium, Sclerotinia, Rhizoctonia
- Seed treatment: 4–6 g/kg seed; Soil application: 2.5 kg/ha with FYM

NUCLEAR POLYHEDROSIS VIRUS (NPV):
- Helicoverpa NPV (HaNPV): 250 LE/ha for bollworm, gram pod borer
- Spodoptera NPV (SlNPV): 250 LE/ha for FAW, tobacco caterpillar
- Apply at egg hatching; 5-7 day interval; do not mix with alkaline sprays

## PESTICIDE SAFETY AND FIRST AID

SAFETY PRECAUTIONS:
- Always read the label before use
- Wear PPE: gloves, goggles, mask, rubber boots, apron
- Never spray during peak sunlight (10 AM – 4 PM in summer)
- Do not eat, drink, or smoke while spraying
- Wash hands thoroughly after handling pesticides
- Keep children and livestock away from sprayed fields

PRE-HARVEST INTERVAL (PHI): Observe waiting period before harvest:
- Organophosphates: 14–21 days
- Pyrethroids: 7–14 days
- Neonicotinoids: 7–21 days
- Triazoles (fungicides): 14–21 days

FIRST AID FOR PESTICIDE POISONING:
- Remove from exposure; remove contaminated clothing
- Wash skin with soap and water; eyes with clean water (15 min)
- If swallowed: Do NOT induce vomiting unless directed by doctor
- Call Poison Control: 1800-11-6117 (India, toll-free)
- Rush to nearest hospital; carry pesticide label
