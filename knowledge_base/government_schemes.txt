GOVERNMENT AGRICULTURE SCHEMES FOR INDIAN FARMERS
==================================================

## PM-KISAN (PRADHAN MANTRI KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI)
LAUNCHED: February 24, 2019
OBJECTIVE: Provide income support to all landholder farmer families

BENEFITS:
- ₹6,000 per year in 3 equal installments of ₹2,000
- Directly transferred to bank account via DBT
- First installment: April–July; Second: August–November; Third: December–March

ELIGIBILITY:
- All landholder farmers with cultivable land
- Excludes: Constitutional post holders, income tax payers, Government employees (except Class IV/Multi-Tasking Staff)
- Small and marginal farmers (< 2 ha) get priority

HOW TO APPLY:
- Visit pm-kisan.gov.in or nearest CSC (Common Service Centre)
- Required documents: Aadhaar, bank account, land records (Khatauni)
- Mobile app: PM Kisan App available on Google Play Store

STATUS: Over 11 crore farmers registered; ₹2.4 lakh crore disbursed (as of 2024)

## FASAL BIMA YOJANA – PMFBY (PRADHAN MANTRI FASAL BIMA YOJANA)
LAUNCHED: January 13, 2016 (Kharif 2016)
OBJECTIVE: Comprehensive crop insurance against natural calamities

PREMIUM RATES (Farmer's share):
- Kharif crops: Maximum 2% of Sum Insured
- Rabi crops: Maximum 1.5% of Sum Insured
- Annual commercial/horticultural crops: Maximum 5%
- Government pays remaining premium (states and Centre share 50:50)

RISKS COVERED:
- Pre-sowing risk: Prevented sowing due to drought, flood
- Standing crop (Sowing to Harvesting): drought, flood, cyclone, storm, hailstorm, landslide, pest, disease, waterlogging
- Post-harvest losses (14 days after harvest): cyclone, unseasonal rain
- Localized calamities: hailstorm, landslide

ENROLLMENT:
- Compulsory for loanee farmers (taking crop loan from banks/MFIs)
- Voluntary for non-loanee farmers
- Enroll through: Bank/KCC account, CSC, Agri-Insurance Portal, PMFBY App

CLAIM PROCESS:
- Damage report within 72 hours (localized) or after crop cutting experiments (CCE)
- Claims settled within 30 days of government notification
- Amount credited directly to bank account

## SOIL HEALTH CARD (SHC) SCHEME
LAUNCHED: February 19, 2015
OBJECTIVE: Promote soil testing and balanced fertilizer use

FEATURES:
- Free soil testing at government and private labs
- Tests 12 soil health parameters (N, P, K, S, Zn, Fe, Cu, Mn, B, pH, EC, OC)
- Crop-specific fertilizer recommendations
- Card valid for 2 years; renewed every crop season in pilot areas
- Available in local language

BENEFITS TO FARMERS:
- Reduces fertilizer cost by 10–20% (no over-application)
- Increases yield by 5–10%
- Improves soil health over time

HOW TO GET:
- Contact nearest Soil Testing Laboratory (Mitti Janch Kendra)
- Call Kisan Call Center: 1800-180-1551 (toll-free)
- App: mySoil Health Card App

## KISAN CREDIT CARD (KCC)
LAUNCHED: August 1998 (Modified in 2004, 2012)
OBJECTIVE: Provide timely and adequate credit for agricultural needs

FEATURES:
- Revolving credit facility for crop cultivation, post-harvest, allied activities
- Interest rate: 7% per annum (with 3% interest subvention for timely repayment = effective 4%)
- Credit limit based on land holding, crop, and scale of finance
- Personal accident insurance: ₹50,000 (death/permanent disability) + ₹25,000 (others)

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES:
- Crop production (short-term needs)
- Post-harvest expenses, marketing
- Farm maintenance (repair of machinery)
- Allied activities: fisheries, animal husbandry, beekeeping

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Aadhaar, PAN
- Land records (Khatauni/Khasra)
- Passport size photos
- Affidavit (no dues certificate)

## PARAMPARAGAT KRISHI VIKAS YOJANA (PKVY)
LAUNCHED: 2015–16
OBJECTIVE: Promote organic farming through cluster approach

FEATURES:
- ₹50,000 per ha for 3 years (₹31,000 to farmers directly)
- Cluster of 50 farmers, 50 acres land minimum
- Organic certification support (PGS-India)
- Market linkage support
- Training and capacity building

COMPONENTS:
1. Jaivik Kheti Portal: portal.pgsindia-ncof.gov.in
2. MOVCD-NE: Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North East
3. Capital Investment Subsidy for units

---

## NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY MISSION (NFSM)
OBJECTIVE: Increase production of rice, wheat, pulses, coarse cereals, oilseeds

INTERVENTIONS:
- Seed distribution of HYV/hybrid varieties at 50% subsidy
- Demonstration of improved technology packages (cluster front-line demonstrations)
- Farm machinery subsidy (power weeders, SRI equipment, manual sprayers)
- Soil ameliorants (lime, gypsum) at 50% subsidy in deficient areas
- Training of farmers and extension workers

CROPS COVERED: Rice, wheat, pulses (tur, moong, urad, masur, groundnut, soybean), coarse cereals (maize, bajra, jowar)

## RASHTRIYA KRISHI VIKAS YOJANA (RKVY-RAFTAAR)
LAUNCHED: 2007 (RAFTAAR added 2017–18)
OBJECTIVE: Incentivize states to invest in agriculture infrastructure

COMPONENTS:
- Agribusiness incubation: ₹25 lakh to ₹2 crore for agri-entrepreneurs
- RAFTAAR (Remunerative Approaches for Agriculture and Allied sector Rejuvenation)
- Farmer Producer Organizations (FPO) support
- Agri-startups: seed funding through RKVY agribusiness incubators

---

## e-NAM (NATIONAL AGRICULTURE MARKET)
LAUNCHED: April 14, 2016
OBJECTIVE: Unified national electronic trading platform for agricultural commodities

FEATURES:
- Online trading of 100+ commodities across 1000+ mandis in 18 states
- Transparent price discovery through competitive bidding
- Farmer gets price from entire country, not just local mandi
- Payment directly to bank account within 24 hours

HOW TO PARTICIPATE:
- Register at enam.gov.in
- Required: Aadhaar, bank account, mobile number
- Can participate through local mandi or from home via mobile app

BENEFITS:
- Farmers get 5–10% higher prices on average
- Reduces intermediary exploitation
- Reduces post-harvest losses through better logistics info

## GKY – GRAMIN KRISHI VIKASH YOJANA (State-level schemes)
Various state governments provide additional support:

ANDHRA PRADESH:
- YSR Free Crop Input Scheme: Free seeds, fertilizers for crop loss
- YSR Rythu Bharosa: ₹13,500/ha/year (in addition to PM-KISAN)

TELANGANA:
- Rythu Bandhu: ₹10,000/ha/season investment support; ₹20,000/year
- Rythu Bima: Free life insurance of ₹5 lakh for all farmers (18–59 years)

MADHYA PRADESH:
- Mukhyamantri Kisan Kalyan Yojana: ₹6,000/year additional to PM-KISAN

KARNATAKA:
- Krishi Bhagya: Farm pond support, micro-irrigation, soil health

MAHARASHTRA:
- Magel Tyala Shet Tale (Farm Pond) scheme

RAJASTHAN:
- Mukhyamantri Krishi Udyog Yojana: Agro-processing subsidy

## CROP MINIMUM SUPPORT PRICE (MSP)

MSP is the government-guaranteed price to protect farmers from market fluctuations.

KHARIF 2024–25 MSP (Sample):
- Paddy (Common): ₹2,300/quintal
- Paddy (Grade A): ₹2,320/quintal
- Jowar (Hybrid): ₹3,371/quintal
- Bajra: ₹2,625/quintal
- Maize: ₹2,225/quintal
- Cotton (Medium staple): ₹7,121/quintal
- Cotton (Long staple): ₹7,521/quintal
- Soybean: ₹4,892/quintal
- Groundnut: ₹6,783/quintal
- Tur (Arhar): ₹7,550/quintal
- Moong: ₹8,682/quintal
- Urad: ₹7,400/quintal

RABI 2024–25 MSP (Sample):
- Wheat: ₹2,275/quintal
- Barley: ₹1,735/quintal
- Gram: ₹5,440/quintal
- Masur (Lentil): ₹6,700/quintal
- Mustard/Rapeseed: ₹5,650/quintal
- Safflower: ₹5,800/quintal

PROCUREMENT AGENCIES:
- Wheat and Rice: FCI (Food Corporation of India), state agencies
- Pulses and Oilseeds: NAFED, NCCF
- Cotton: CCI (Cotton Corporation of India)
